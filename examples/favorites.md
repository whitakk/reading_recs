## The Bitter Lesson — Rich Sutton
http://www.incompleteideas.net/IncIdeas/BitterLesson.html

The biggest lesson from 70 years of AI research is that general methods leveraging computation are ultimately the most effective. Researchers have repeatedly tried to build in human knowledge, but search and learning methods that scale with compute always win in the long run.

## Software 2.0 — Andrej Karpathy
https://karpathy.medium.com/software-2-0-a64152b37c35

Neural networks represent a fundamental shift in how we write software. Instead of explicit programming, we specify goals and optimization, and the network writes the program. This isn't just ML — it's a new programming paradigm that will eat traditional software.
